Step One: Pick the Right Tool Before You Write a Single Prompt

The single biggest mistake beginners make is starting with the wrong tool. A prompt that produces a stunning image on Midjourney might generate something mediocre on DALL-E 3, and vice versa. Different tools have different strengths, pricing structures, and interfaces. Here's the honest breakdown for 2026:

Midjourney v6.1

From $10/mo

Produces the highest aesthetic quality of any tool — especially portraits, landscapes, and cinematic scenes. Uses Discord or a web interface. Has a learning curve but rewards it. Best for creators who care about visual artistry above all else.

DALL-E 3 (via ChatGPT)

$20/mo (Plus)

The most beginner-friendly tool because it understands plain English instructions and lets you refine results conversationally. Best for people who want results quickly without studying prompt syntax. Included in ChatGPT Plus.

Leonardo AI

Free / Paid

The best free option in 2026. 150 daily credits, no watermarks, multiple specialized models, and a clean web interface. PhotoReal v2 produces genuinely impressive photorealistic results at zero cost. Start here if you're not ready to pay.

Adobe Firefly 3

Free / CC

The only major AI image generator trained exclusively on licensed Adobe Stock images and public domain content. This makes it the only genuinely commercially safe choice. Essential for agencies, designers, and anyone selling AI-generated work.

Stable Diffusion XL (Local)

Free

Runs on your own hardware with no monthly fee, no usage limits, and complete privacy. Requires a capable GPU (8GB VRAM minimum) and some technical setup. Maximum creative control via ControlNet, LoRA fine-tuning, and custom models. Best for developers and power users.

Ideogram 2.0

Free / Paid

The only reliable tool for generating images that contain readable, accurate text. Essential for creating social media graphics, poster designs, merchandise mockups, and thumbnails where text must be legible. Other tools still struggle with this.

Quick Decision Rule

Just starting out and want free? → Leonardo AI. Want the easiest experience? → DALL-E 3 via ChatGPT Plus. Want maximum quality and willing to learn? → Midjourney. Need commercial safety guarantees? → Adobe Firefly. Need text inside images? → Ideogram 2.0.

Generating Your First Image: Step-by-Step on Each Platform

Leonardo AI — Your First Free Generation

01
Create your free account

Go to leonardo.ai and sign up with Google or email. No credit card needed. You receive 150 tokens per day — a standard 512×512 image costs around 3 tokens, giving you roughly 50 free images per day.

02
Select a model that matches your intended style

This is the most important choice you'll make. Use PhotoReal v2 for realistic photography-style images. Use Leonardo Diffusion XL for painterly or illustrated art. Use Kino XL for cinematic and editorial visuals. Use Anime Pastel Dream for anime or manga style. Selecting the wrong model is the #1 cause of disappointing results.

03
Write your prompt and set image count to 4

Always generate 4 images at once for your first attempt on any new prompt. This gives you variation to compare. Enter your prompt in the text box — we'll cover exactly how to write prompts in the next section. Set dimensions to match your target use case before generating.

04
Enable Alchemy for higher quality (optional)

The Alchemy toggle adds a refinement pass that noticeably improves sharpness, coherence, and detail — at the cost of roughly 2× tokens. Worth enabling once you have a prompt you're happy with and want to polish the result.

05
Upscale and download your best result

Click your favourite image and select the Upscale button (2× or 4×). This uses AI to increase resolution while preserving detail. Download as PNG for maximum quality. For web use, export as WebP to reduce file size.

Midjourney — Your First Paid Generation

01
Subscribe and access via Discord or the web app

Visit midjourney.com and subscribe — Basic ($10/month for 200 generations), Standard ($30/month for unlimited relaxed), or Pro ($60/month for stealth mode + faster queue). The Standard plan is the most popular. You can use the newer midjourney.com web interface or the original Discord bot.

02
Use /imagine to generate your first image

In Discord, type /imagine and write your prompt. Midjourney generates a 2×2 grid of four variations. Buttons U1–U4 upscale a specific variation. Buttons V1–V4 create new variations based on that image. The 🔄 button regenerates all four from scratch.

03
Add parameters to control the output

Parameters go at the end of your prompt after double dashes. Key ones: --ar 16:9 sets aspect ratio, --style raw reduces artistic stylization for more photorealistic results, --chaos 25 increases variation between the four images, --s 200 sets stylization strength (0–1000), --no text, watermark, blur acts as a negative prompt.

04
Use Vary (Subtle) or Vary (Strong) to iterate

Once you upscale an image you like, you'll see Vary (Subtle) and Vary (Strong) buttons. Subtle keeps the composition and makes small changes. Strong makes significant variations. This iterative process is how you converge on exactly the image you want without starting over from scratch.

The Prompt System That Works on Every Platform

Most guides tell you to "be descriptive." That's not actually helpful. Here is a repeatable system that produces strong results on Midjourney, Leonardo, DALL-E 3, and Stable Diffusion — every time.

Think of every prompt as having six layers. You don't need all six for every image, but the more layers you include, the closer your output will be to what you actually imagined.

The 6-Layer Prompt System

Layer 1 — Subject: Who or what is the main focus? Be specific about age, appearance, clothing, expression.

Layer 2 — Action: What is the subject doing? A static subject is fine; an active one creates more dynamic images.

Layer 3 — Setting: Where is this happening? Time of day, location, weather, season all matter enormously.

Layer 4 — Lighting: This single element changes the mood of an image more than almost anything else. Golden hour, overcast flat light, neon glow, candlelight, studio softbox — all produce completely different feelings.

Layer 5 — Style: Photography, oil painting, watercolour, concept art, editorial illustration, 3D render — specify the visual language you want.

Layer 6 — Technical specs: Camera type, lens focal length, depth of field, film grain, rendering quality. These make AI images look genuinely professional.

Negative Prompts: What to Always Exclude

Negative prompts tell the AI what to avoid. On Leonardo AI and Stable Diffusion, there's a dedicated negative prompt field. On Midjourney, use --no at the end of your prompt.

Universal Negative Prompt — Copy and Use blurry, out of focus, low quality, pixelated, distorted, deformed, extra limbs, extra fingers, mutated hands, watermark, text, signature, username, logo, cropped, jpeg artifacts, oversaturated, bad anatomy, poorly drawn face, ugly, duplicate, cloned face, gross proportions

Key Settings Decoded: Stop Guessing, Start Understanding

Every AI image generator has settings that look intimidating. They're not. Here's exactly what each one does and what to set it to:

CFG / Guidance Scale How strictly to follow your prompt

Low values (3–5) = creative, unpredictable. High values (7–12) = follows your prompt strictly. Start at 7. If the image drifts away from your description, increase to 8–10. If it looks unnatural, decrease to 6.

Sampling Steps Refinement passes

More steps = more detail but slower generation. 20 steps = fast and decent. 30–40 = the sweet spot for quality vs. speed. Above 50 shows diminishing returns. For final outputs, use 35; for quick tests, use 20.

Seed Number Reproducibility lock

The same seed + same prompt = identical image every time. Invaluable for creating consistent characters across multiple images. Always save the seed number of any image you love before closing the tab.

Aspect Ratio Image shape

1:1 (square) for profile pictures and social posts. 16:9 for YouTube thumbnails and website banners. 4:5 for Instagram feed. 9:16 for Stories, TikTok, and Reels. 3:2 for blog feature images. Always set this before generating.

Sampler / Scheduler Generation algorithm

DPM++ 2M Karras is the safest default — reliable, detailed, good with all prompt types. Euler a is faster but sometimes less detailed. DDIM is good for img2img. Unless you have a specific reason, stay on DPM++ 2M Karras.

Denoising Strength (img2img) How much to change your input image

0.3 = very similar to input, subtle changes. 0.5 = moderate transformation, keeps structure. 0.7+ = significant creative departure. For consistency workflows, stay at 0.35–0.5.

5 Mistakes That Ruin AI Images (and How to Fix Them)

Mistake 01 Using vague, generic prompts

"A beautiful landscape" gives the AI nothing to work with. Always specify the location, time of day, weather, lighting quality, and mood. Specificity is the difference between stock-looking and stunning.

Mistake 02 Wrong model for the style

Using a photorealism model for watercolour art, or a stylised art model for product photography, produces off-brand results no matter how good your prompt is. Model selection comes before prompt writing.

Mistake 03 Ignoring the negative prompt

Not using negative prompts means the AI can introduce blurriness, distortion, watermarks, and anatomical errors freely. The universal negative prompt at the end of this article prevents 80% of common issues.

Mistake 04 Generating one image and stopping

Always generate at least 4 variations per prompt. AI image generation has inherent randomness — sometimes the 4th output is dramatically better than the 1st. Treat each generation run as a batch, not a single attempt.

Mistake 05 Not saving seed numbers

When you get an image you love, save the seed number immediately. Without it, you can never reproduce that exact result. Seeds are how professional workflows maintain visual consistency across entire content campaigns.

3 Advanced Workflows for Professional Results

Workflow 1 — Consistent Character Across a Content Series

The hardest challenge in AI image generation is keeping a character looking the same across multiple images. This workflow solves it without needing expensive tools:

  1. Generate your character and find one image that perfectly captures how they should look. Download it and save the seed number.
  2. On Midjourney, use --cref [image URL] to set a character reference. The AI will maintain key facial and physical features across new prompts. Adjust --cw (character weight, 0–100) to control how strictly it adheres to the reference.
  3. On Leonardo AI, use the Image Guidance feature — upload your reference image and set the guidance strength to 0.4–0.6. Higher values maintain stronger visual similarity at the cost of compositional flexibility.
  4. Keep the character description identical in every prompt — same hair, same eye colour, same distinctive features. Only change the setting, action, and lighting.
  5. Generate in batches: create all outdoor scenes, then all indoor scenes, then all close-ups. This keeps the generation context consistent within each batch.

Workflow 2 — Brand Visual Content at Scale

Marketing teams generating dozens of on-brand images per week use this system to maintain visual consistency across every asset:

  1. Define your brand's visual DNA in prompt language. Write down the exact lighting style, colour palette, compositional rules, photography style, and mood you want. Example: "warm amber and terracotta tones, soft diffused natural lighting, lifestyle photography aesthetic, authentic and approachable, Sony A7 35mm."
  2. Create one master prompt template with placeholders: [SUBJECT], [ACTION], [SETTING], warm amber and terracotta tones, soft diffused natural lighting, lifestyle photography aesthetic, authentic and approachable, Sony A7 35mm, photorealistic, 4K.
  3. Swap only the [SUBJECT], [ACTION], and [SETTING] variables for each new image. Every other element stays identical. This is what creates visual brand consistency across hundreds of images.
  4. Run a test batch of 20 images using this template and review for outliers. Add specific exclusions to your negative prompt for any recurring issues.
  5. Archive all seed numbers from approved outputs. This lets you regenerate any image if the original file is lost.

Workflow 3 — Product Lifestyle Images for E-Commerce

E-commerce brands use this approach to generate unlimited product lifestyle photography without a single photo shoot:

  1. Start with a clean product shot: white or transparent background, well-lit, high resolution. This is your source image.
  2. Use img2img or inpainting on Leonardo AI or Stable Diffusion to composite your product into generated lifestyle environments. Keep denoising strength at 0.35–0.45 to preserve the product's appearance.
  3. Build a library of 10–15 "scene templates" matching your brand aesthetics: morning kitchen counter, outdoor café table, luxury marble bathroom, cosy bedroom nightstand, urban workspace desktop.
  4. Run each product through each scene template. This produces a full lifestyle image library automatically. For a catalogue of 20 products and 10 scenes, you can generate 200 unique lifestyle images in under an hour.
  5. Apply a consistent colour grade in post-processing using Adobe Lightroom presets or Canva to unify the visual look across all generated images.

Copyright and Commercial Use — What You Actually Need to Know

Before publishing or selling AI-generated images, understand these four things:

  • Most tools allow commercial use on paid plans. Midjourney paid plans, DALL-E 3 via ChatGPT Plus, and Leonardo AI paid plans all permit commercial use. Free tiers often do not — always read the current terms of service.
  • Adobe Firefly is the only commercially clean option. It was trained exclusively on licensed Adobe Stock content and public domain works. If you're an agency creating work for clients, or a brand creating advertising, Firefly is the legally safest choice.
  • Disclosure is increasingly required. Multiple jurisdictions now require disclosure of AI-generated content in advertising, journalism, and political communications. Best practice is to disclose proactively regardless of legal requirement.
  • Generating realistic fake images of real people is a legal risk. Most platforms ban this in their terms of service, and deepfake laws are now active in multiple countries. Avoid this entirely.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which AI image generator gives the best results for free? +

Leonardo AI offers the best free tier in 2026 — 150 daily credits, no watermarks, and multiple specialized models including PhotoReal v2. Adobe Firefly is also free with a Creative Cloud account and is the safest choice for commercial use. Both are excellent starting points with no payment required.

Why do my AI-generated images look blurry or wrong? +

The three most common causes: (1) Vague prompt — add specific details about lighting, style, composition, and technical specs. (2) Wrong model — a photorealism model interprets "watercolour" differently than an illustration model. Match your model to your target style. (3) CFG/guidance scale too low — raise to 7–9 to make the AI follow your instructions more closely.

Can I sell AI-generated images commercially? +

Yes, on most paid platforms. Midjourney paid plans, DALL-E 3 via ChatGPT Plus, and Adobe Firefly all allow commercial use. Adobe Firefly provides the strongest legal protection as it was trained only on licensed content. Always check the current terms of service before using images commercially — policies update regularly.

What is the best prompt format for AI image generators? +

The most reliable structure: Subject + Action + Setting + Lighting + Style + Camera specs. Example: "A barista making latte art, leaning over the counter, cosy independent coffee shop, golden hour light through glass windows, editorial photography style, shot on Canon EF 50mm f/1.4, shallow depth of field, 4K." Always add a negative prompt to exclude common quality issues like blur, watermarks, and distorted anatomy.

How long does it take to get good at AI image generation? +

Most people reach a solid intermediate level within 2–3 weeks of daily practice. The fastest path: generate 30–50 images daily for the first two weeks on Leonardo AI free, study why specific prompts worked and others didn't, then start applying negative prompts and the 6-layer prompt system. Add one advanced technique per week — img2img, then ControlNet, then style references. Within 30 days of consistent practice, your results will be indistinguishable from professional stock photography.
